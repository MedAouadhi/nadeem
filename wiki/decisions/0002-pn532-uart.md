# 0002 — PN532 v3 NFC reader on UART1 (HSU mode)

**Status:** accepted
**Date:** 2026-04-25 (recorded)

## Context

Need an ISO14443A reader for Mifare-style NFC tags ("semsem"). Three transport options on PN532: I2C, SPI, HSU (UART). I2C is shared with codecs + TCA9555 — adding more devices risks bus contention. SPI requires more pins.

## Decision

Wire PN532 v3 via UART1 (HSU). SEL0 + SEL1 to GND. TX↔GPIO 17 (UART1 RX), RX↔GPIO 18 (UART1 TX). IRQ on GPIO 8 wired but unused (reserved for future ISR-driven polling). 3.3 V power; **never 5 V**.

## Consequences

- Dedicated UART, no contention with other I2C peripherals.
- Polling-mode driver in `firmware/components/nfc_pn532/`. Simpler than ISR; wakes once per cycle.
- IRQ wire is "future-proofing" — when adopted, polling task can become ISR-driven and reduce idle CPU.
- 3.3 V constraint = breakout module must have its level-shifter respected.
